# Bile Duct Cancer Causes and Risk Factors

Anything that increases your chance of getting a disease is called a risk factor. There are several risk factors associated with [bile duct cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=527370&version=patient&language=English&dictionary=Cancer.gov). Not everyone with one or more of these risk factors will develop the disease, and the disease will develop in some people who donâ€™t have any known risk factors. People who think they may be at risk should discuss this with their doctor.

Risk factors for bile duct cancer include the following conditions:

- primary sclerosing cholangitis (a [progressive disease](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45850&version=patient&language=English&dictionary=Cancer.gov) in which the [bile ducts](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46738&version=patient&language=English&dictionary=Cancer.gov) become blocked by [inflammation](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44042&version=patient&language=English&dictionary=Cancer.gov) and scarring)
- chronic [ulcerative colitis](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45493&version=patient&language=English&dictionary=Cancer.gov)
- [cysts](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46461&version=patient&language=English&dictionary=Cancer.gov) in the bile ducts (cysts block the flow of [bile](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46508&version=patient&language=English&dictionary=Cancer.gov) and can cause swollen bile ducts, inflammation, and [infection](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45364&version=patient&language=English&dictionary=Cancer.gov))
- infection with a Chinese liver fluke [parasite](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44310&version=patient&language=English&dictionary=Cancer.gov)

There are actions you can take that can help to decrease your risk of getting cancer. To learn more about ways to prevent cancer, see [Cancer Prevention Overview](/about-cancer/causes-prevention/patient-prevention-overview-pdq).
