## Use in Cancer

Hydroxyurea
is approved to treat:

- **[Chronic myelogenous leukemia](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44901&version=Patient&language=English)** that is [refractory](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=350245&version=Patient&language=English) (does not [respond](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44085&version=Patient&language=English) to treatment).
- **[Squamous cell carcinoma of the head and neck](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=597171&version=Patient&language=English)** (excluding the lip) that is [locally advanced](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45955&version=Patient&language=English). It is used with [chemoradiation](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=306495&version=Patient&language=English).

The use of hydroxyurea to treat [cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45333&version=Patient&language=English) is approved for the Hydrea brand. Hydroxyurea is also approved to treat [sickle cell anemia](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=559458&version=Patient&language=English). This use is approved for the Droxia brand.

Hydroxyurea
is also being studied in the treatment of other [conditions](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=651193&version=Patient&language=English) and types of
cancer.
