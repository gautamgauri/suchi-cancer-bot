## Use in Cancer

Pralsetinib
is approved to treat:

- **[Non-small cell lung cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45323&version=Patient&language=English)** that has a *RET* [fusion gene](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=613509&version=Patient&language=English) and has spread to other parts of the body. It is used in adults.
- **[Thyroid cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=445091&version=Patient&language=English)** that has a *RET* fusion gene and has spread. It is used in adults and children aged 12 years and older who need [systemic therapy](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45922&version=Patient&language=English), including those who received [radioactive iodine](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45855&version=Patient&language=English) and it did not work or is no longer working.¹

¹This use is approved under FDA’s [Accelerated Approval Program](https://www.fda.gov/ForPatients/Approvals/Fast/ucm405447.htm). As a condition of approval, [confirmatory trial(s)](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=799146&version=Patient&language=English) must show that pralsetinib provides a clinical benefit in these patients.

Pralsetinib
is also being studied in the treatment of other types of
cancer.
