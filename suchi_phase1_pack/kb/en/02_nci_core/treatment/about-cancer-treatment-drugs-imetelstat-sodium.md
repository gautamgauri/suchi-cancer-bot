# Imetelstat Sodium

Placeholder slot

(ih-MEH-tel-stat SOH-dee-um)

Imetelstat sodium works by blocking the activity of an enzyme called telomerase, which is found at high levels in cells that divide rapidly, including cancer cells. Blocking this enzyme keeps cancer cells from dividing and causes them to die. Imetelstat sodiumÂ is a type of targeted therapy called an enzyme inhibitor.

## Use in Cancer

Imetelstat sodium
is approved to treat:

- **[Myelodysplastic syndrome](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45266&version=Patient&language=English)** (MDS) that is low risk or intermediate-1 risk. It is used in adults who:
- have [anemia](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45360&version=Patient&language=English) that required blood transfusions of 4 or more [red blood cell](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46124&version=Patient&language=English) units over 8 weeks, and
- have not responded to, have stopped responding to, or cannot be treated with an [erythropoiesis-stimulating agent](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=747357&version=Patient&language=English).

Imetelstat sodium
is also being studied in the treatment of other types of
cancer.
