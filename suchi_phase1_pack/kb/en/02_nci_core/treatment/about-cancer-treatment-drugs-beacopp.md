This combination contains the chemotherapy drugs bleomycin, etoposide phosphate, doxorubicin hydrochloride (Adriamycin), cyclophophamide, vincristine sulfate (Oncovin), and procarbazine hydrochloride and the steroid hormone prednisone. Combinations usually work better than single drugs because
different drugs kill
cancer
cells in different ways.

Drugs in the BEACOPP combination:

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **B** | = [Bleomycin](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/bleomycin) |
| **E** | = [Etoposide Phosphate](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/etoposidephosphate) |
| **A** | = [Doxorubicin Hydrochloride](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/doxorubicinhydrochloride) (Adriamycin) |
| **C** | = [Cyclophosphamide](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/cyclophosphamide) |
| **O** | = [Vincristine Sulfate](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/vincristinesulfate) (Oncovin) |
| **P** | = [Procarbazine Hydrochloride](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/procarbazinehydrochloride) |
| **P** | = [Prednisone](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/prednisone) |

Each of the drugs in this combination is approved by the [Food and Drug Administration (FDA)](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=454785&version=Patient&language=English) to treat cancer or conditions related to cancer.

## Use in Cancer

BEACOPP is used to treat:

- **[Hodgkin lymphoma](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=270800&version=Patient&language=English).**

This combination may also be used with other drugs or treatments or to treat other types of cancer.
