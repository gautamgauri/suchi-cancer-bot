# Abemaciclib - NCI

# Abemaciclib

Placeholder slot

(uh-BEH-muh-SY-klib)

Abemaciclib works by blocking the activity of proteins called CDK4 and CDK6 that control how fast cells grow. Blocking these proteins may slow or stop the growth of cancer cells. Abemaciclib is a type of targeted therapy drug called a kinase inhibitor. It is a small-molecule drug (a drug that can enter cells easily).

## Use in Cancer

Abemaciclib
is approved to be used alone or with other drugs to treat:

- **[Breast cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=444971&version=Patient&language=English)** that is [hormone receptor positive](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=788029&version=Patient&language=English) (HR+) and [HER2 negative](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=774531&version=Patient&language=English) (HER2-).
- In adults with [early-stage breast cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=446564&version=Patient&language=English) that has spread to the [lymph nodes](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45762&version=Patient&language=English) and has a high risk of coming back, it is used with [tamoxifen citrate](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=509341&version=Patient&language=English) or an [aromatase inhibitor](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44232&version=Patient&language=English) as [adjuvant therapy](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45587&version=Patient&language=English).
- In adults whose cancer has spread ([advanced](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=478743&version=Patient&language=English) or [metastatic](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44058&version=Patient&language=English)). It is used:
- with [fulvestrant](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=306498&version=Patient&language=English) for cancer that got worse after treatment with [hormone therapy](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45110&version=Patient&language=English)
- alone for cancer that got worse after treatment with hormone therapy and chemotherapy given for [metastatic](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44058&version=Patient&language=English) cancer
- with an aromatase inhibitor given as [first-line](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=346494&version=Patient&language=English) hormone therapy in men and [postmenopausal](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45269&version=Patient&language=English) women

Abemaciclib
is also being studied in the treatment of other types of
cancer.
