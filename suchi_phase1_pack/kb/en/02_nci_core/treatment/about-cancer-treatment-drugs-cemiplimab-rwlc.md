# Cemiplimab-rwlc - NCI

# Cemiplimab-rwlc

Placeholder slot

(seh-MIP-lih-mab)

Cemiplimab-rwlc is a type of drug called an immune checkpoint inhibitor (a type of immunotherapy). It is a monoclonal antibody that binds to the protein PD-1 on the surface of immune cells called T cells. It works by keeping cancer cells from suppressing the immune system. This allows the immune system to attack and kill the cancer cells.

## Use in Cancer

Cemiplimab-rwlc
is approved to treat:

- **[Basal cell carcinoma](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46515&version=Patient&language=English)** **(BCC)** (a type of skin cancer). It is used in patients whose cancer cannot be removed by surgery or has spread after treatment with [hedgehog pathway](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=811118&version=Patient&language=English) inhibitor therapy or if hedgehog pathway inhibitor therapy cannot be used.
- **[Cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=795104&version=Patient&language=English)** (a type of skin cancer) that has spread or cannot be treated with surgery or [radiation therapy](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44971&version=Patient&language=English).
- **[Non-small cell lung cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45323&version=Patient&language=English).** It is used in adults whose cancer has not spread outside the chest and cannot be treated with surgery or chemotherapy and radiation therapy or has spread to other parts of the body. It is used:
- Alone as the first treatment when the cancer has the [PD-L1](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=797389&version=Patient&language=English) [protein](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46092&version=Patient&language=English) but does not have a [mutation](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46063&version=Patient&language=English) in the *[EGFR](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44397&version=Patient&language=English)*, *[ALK](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=721252&version=Patient&language=English)*, or *[ROS1](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=779660&version=Patient&language=English)* gene.
- With [platinum](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45841&version=Patient&language=English) chemotherapy as the first treatment when the cancer does not have a mutation in the *EGFR*, *ALK*, or *ROS1* gene.

Cemiplimab-rwlc
is also being studied in the treatment of other types of
cancer.
