## Use in Cancer

Cisplatin is approved to be used alone or with other drugs to treat:

- **[Bladder cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=444968&version=Patient&language=English).** It is used alone in patients with [advanced](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=478743&version=Patient&language=English) cancer that cannot be treated with other therapies, such as surgery or [radiation therapy](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44971&version=Patient&language=English).
- **[Ovarian cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=445074&version=Patient&language=English)** that has spread to other parts of the body. It is used with other drugs in patients who have already had surgery or radiation therapy. It is used alone in patients whose cancer has not gotten better with [standard](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44930&version=Patient&language=English) chemotherapy and who have not received previous cisplatin.
- **[Testicular cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=445090&version=Patient&language=English)** that has spread to other parts of the body. It is used with other drugs in patients who have already had surgery or radiation therapy.

Cisplatin is also being studied in the treatment of other types of cancer.
