## Use in Cancer

Granisetron hydrochloride
is approved to [prevent](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=439419&version=Patient&language=English):

- **[Nausea](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=390302&version=Patient&language=English) and [vomiting](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=390324&version=Patient&language=English)** caused by [chemotherapy](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45214&version=Patient&language=English) and [radiation therapy](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44971&version=Patient&language=English).

Granisetron hydrochloride
is approved to [prevent](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=439419&version=Patient&language=English) and treat:

- **[Nausea](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=390302&version=Patient&language=English) and [vomiting](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=390324&version=Patient&language=English)** after [surgery](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45570&version=Patient&language=English) in adults.

Granisetron hydrochloride
is also being studied in the treatment of other [conditions](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=651193&version=Patient&language=English).

Granisetron hydrochloride is also available in a different form called [granisetron](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45432&version=Patient&language=English). For more information, see the Drug Information Summary for [Granisetron](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/granisetron).
