# MVAC - NCI

# MVAC

This chemotherapy combination contains the drugs methotrexate, vinblastine sulfate, doxorubicin hydrochloride, and cisplatin. Combinations usually work better than single drugs because different drugs kill cancer cells in different ways.

Drugs in the MVAC combination:

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **M** | = [Methotrexate](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/methotrexate-sodium) |
| **V** | = [Vinblastine Sulfate](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/vinblastinesulfate) |
| **A** | = [Doxorubicin Hydrochloride](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/doxorubicinhydrochloride) (Adriamycin) |
| **C** | = [Cisplatin](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/cisplatin) |

Each of the drugs in this combination is approved by the
[Food and Drug
Administration (FDA)](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=454785&version=Patient&language=English)
to treat cancer or conditions related to cancer.

## Use in Cancer

MVAC
is used to treat:

- **[Urothelial carcinoma](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=783188&version=Patient&language=English)**, including [bladder cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=444968&version=Patient&language=English), that is [advanced](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=478743&version=Patient&language=English).

This combination may also be used with other drugs or
treatments or to treat other types of cancer.
