# Topotecan Hydrochloride

(toh-poh-TEE-kan HY-droh-KLOR-ide)

Topotecan hydrochloride is a chemotherapy drug that damages the DNA of cancer cells and other rapidly growing cells, causing them to die.

## Use in Cancer

Topotecan hydrochloride is approved to be used alone or with other drugs to treat:

- **[Cervical cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=444973&version=Patient&language=English)** that has spread to other parts of the body, has come back, or cannot be cured with other therapy. It is used with [cisplatin](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45230&version=Patient&language=English).
- **[Ovarian cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=445074&version=Patient&language=English)** that has spread to other parts of the body. It is used alone in patients whose cancer got worse during or after other chemotherapy.
- **[Small cell lung cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45327&version=Patient&language=English)** that is [platinum sensitive](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=794132&version=Patient&language=English). It is used alone in patients whose cancer got worse after [first-line](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=346494&version=Patient&language=English) chemotherapy.

Topotecan hydrochloride is also being studied in the treatment of other types of cancer.
