# Pamidronate Disodium - NCI

# Pamidronate Disodium

Placeholder slot

(puh-MIH-droh-nayt dy-SOH-dee-um)

Pamidronate disodium works by slowing the breakdown of bone, increasing bone density, and decreasing the amount of calcium released from the bones into the blood. It is a type of drug called a bisphosphonate.

US Brand Name(s)

Aredia

FDA Approved

Yes

[FDA label information for this drug is available at DailyMed.](https://dailymed.nlm.nih.gov/dailymed/search.cfm?labeltype=all&query=PAMIDRONATE+DISODIUM&pagesize=20&page=1&audience=consumer)

## Use in Cancer

Pamidronate disodium
is approved
to be given with chemotherapy to treat bone damage caused by:

- **[breast cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=444971&version=Patient&language=English)** that has spread to bone
- **[multiple myeloma](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45793&version=Patient&language=English)** that has spread to bone

Pamidronate disodium is also approved to treat:

- **[hypercalcemia](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45363&version=Patient&language=English)** (high blood levels of [calcium](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45632&version=Patient&language=English)) caused by [malignant](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45772&version=Patient&language=English) cancer

Pamidronate disodium
is also being studied in the treatment of other types of
cancer.
