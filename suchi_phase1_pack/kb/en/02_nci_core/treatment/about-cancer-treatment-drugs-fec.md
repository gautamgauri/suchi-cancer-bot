# FEC - NCI

# FEC

This chemotherapy combination contains the drugs fluorouracil, epirubicin hydrochloride, and cyclophosphamide. Combinations usually work better than single drugs because
different drugs kill
cancer
cells in different ways.

Drugs in the FEC combination:

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **F** | = [Fluorouracil](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/fluorouracil) |
| **E** | = [Epirubicin Hydrochloride](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/epirubicinhydrochloride) |
| **C** | = [Cyclophosphamide](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/cyclophosphamide) |

Each of the
drugs
in this
combination
is approved by the
[Food and Drug
Administration (FDA)](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=454785&version=Patient&language=English)
to treat
cancer
or conditions related to cancer.

## Use in Cancer

FEC is used to treat:

- **[Breast cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=444971&version=Patient&language=English).**

FEC is also known as CEF. This combination may also be used with other drugs or treatments or to treat other types of cancer.
