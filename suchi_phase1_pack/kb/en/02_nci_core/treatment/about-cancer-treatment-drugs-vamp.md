# VAMP

This combination contains the chemotherapy drugs vincristine sulfate, doxorubicin hydrochloride (Adriamycin), and methotrexate and the steroid hormone prednisone. Combinations usually work better than single drugs because
different drugs kill
cancer
cells in different ways.

Drugs in the VAMP combination:

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **V** | = [Vincristine Sulfate](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/vincristinesulfate) |
| **A** | = [Doxorubicin Hydrochloride](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/doxorubicinhydrochloride) (Adriamycin) |
| **M** | = [Methotrexate](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/methotrexate-sodium) |
| **P** | = [Prednisone](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/prednisone) |

Each of the drugs in this combination is approved by the
[Food and Drug
Administration (FDA)](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=454785&version=Patient&language=English)
to treat cancer or conditions related to cancer.

## Use in Cancer

VAMP
is used to treat:

- **[Hodgkin lymphoma](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=270800&version=Patient&language=English)** in children. It is used with [radiation therapy](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44971&version=Patient&language=English) for cancer that is [low risk](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=794093&version=Patient&language=English).

This combination may also be used with other drugs or treatments or to treat other types of cancer.
