## Use in Cancer

Asparaginase erwinia chrysanthemi (recombinant)-rywn
is approved to treat adults and children aged 1 month and older with:

- **[Acute lymphoblastic leukemia](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45586&version=Patient&language=English).**
- **[Lymphoblastic lymphoma](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=597158&version=Patient&language=English).**

Asparaginase erwinia chrysanthemi (recombinant)-rywn is used in patients who can no longer take asparaginase made from *E. coli*. It is a [recombinant](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44619&version=Patient&language=English) form of [asparaginase](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45339&version=Patient&language=English) made from the [bacterium](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44123&version=Patient&language=English) *Erwinia chrysanthemi*.

This drug is also available in a non-recombinant form. For more information, see the Drug Information Summary for [Asparaginase Erwinia Chrysanthemi](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/asparaginase-erwinia-chrysanthemi).
