## Use in Cancer

Pomalidomide
is approved
to treat:

- **[AIDS-related](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=444964&version=Patient&language=English) [Kaposi sarcoma](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45134&version=Patient&language=English)** in adults whose disease has gotten worse after treatment with [highly active antiretroviral therapy](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=306504&version=Patient&language=English) (HAART).¹
- **Kaposi sarcoma** in adults who are [HIV negative](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=758358&version=Patient&language=English).¹
- **[Multiple myeloma](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45793&version=Patient&language=English).** It is used with [dexamethasone](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45262&version=Patient&language=English) to treat adults whose disease has gotten worse during or after treatment with at least two other [therapies](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44737&version=Patient&language=English) that included [lenalidomide](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=393761&version=Patient&language=English) and a [proteasome inhibitor](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=393541&version=Patient&language=English).

¹This use is approved under FDA’s [Accelerated Approval Program](https://www.fda.gov/ForPatients/Approvals/Fast/ucm405447.htm). As a condition of approval, [confirmatory trial(s)](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=799146&version=Patient&language=English) must show that pomalidomide provides a clinical benefit in these patients.

Pomalidomide
is only available as part of a special program called Pomalyst REMS ([Risk Evaluation and Mitigation Strategies](http://www.pomalystrems.com/)).

Pomalidomide is also being studied in the treatment of other types of
[cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45333&version=Patient&language=English).
