## Use in Cancer

Niraparib tosylate monohydrate and abiraterone acetate is approved to be used with [prednisone](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45492&version=Patient&language=English) to treat:

- **[Prostate cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=445079&version=Patient&language=English)** that has spread to other parts of the body and is [castrate resistant](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=753525&version=Patient&language=English) (has not responded to treatments that lower [testosterone](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45581&version=Patient&language=English) levels). It is used in adults who have certain [mutations](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46063&version=Patient&language=English) in the *[BRCA1](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46047&version=Patient&language=English)* or *[BRCA2](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46742&version=Patient&language=English)* gene.

Niraparib tosylate monohydrate and abiraterone acetate is a fixed-dose combination of niraparib tosylate monohydrate and abiraterone acetate. This form may work better than either drug alone. For more information that may apply to these drugs, see the Drug Information Summaries for [Niraparib Tosylate Monohydrate](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/niraparibtosylatemonohydrate) and [Abiraterone Acetate](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/abirateroneacetate).
