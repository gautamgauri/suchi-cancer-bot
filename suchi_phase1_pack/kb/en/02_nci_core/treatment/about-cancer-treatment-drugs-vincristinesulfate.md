# Vincristine Sulfate - NCI

# Vincristine Sulfate

Placeholder slot

(vin-KRIS-teen SUL-fayt)

Vincristine is a type of chemotherapy called a vinca alkaloid. Vinca alkaloids interfere with microtubules (cellular structures that help move chromosomes during mitosis). They block cell growth by stopping mitosis (cell division).

FDA Approved

Yes

## Use in Cancer

Vincristine sulfate is approved to treat adults and children with:

- **[Acute leukemia](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45145&version=Patient&language=English).**

Vincristine sulfate is sometimes used to treat adults and children with other types of cancer, including:

- **[Hodgkin lymphoma](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=270800&version=Patient&language=English).**
- **[Neuroblastoma](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45418&version=Patient&language=English).**
- **[Non-Hodgkin lymphoma](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45148&version=Patient&language=English)** (NHL).
- **[Rhabdomyosarcoma](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45872&version=Patient&language=English).**
- **[Wilms tumor](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45946&version=Patient&language=English).**

Vincristine sulfate is also being studied in the treatment of other types of cancer.
