## Use in Cancer

Selpercatinib
is approved to treat:

- **[Medullary thyroid cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44550&version=Patient&language=English)** that has an abnormal *[RET](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=810835&version=Patient&language=English)* gene and has spread. It is used
in adults and children aged 2 years and older who need [systemic therapy](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45922&version=Patient&language=English).
- **[Non-small cell lung cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45323&version=Patient&language=English)** that has a *RET* [fusion gene](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=613509&version=Patient&language=English) and has spread. It is used in adults.
- **[Solid tumors](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45301&version=Patient&language=English)** that have a *RET* fusion gene and have spread. It is used in adults and children aged 2 years and older whose cancer got worse during or after systemic therapy or whose cancer cannot be treated with other therapies.¹
- **[Thyroid cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=445091&version=Patient&language=English)** that has a *RET* fusion gene and has spread. It is used in adults and children aged 2 years and older who need systemic therapy, including those who received [radioactive iodine](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45855&version=Patient&language=English) and it did not work or is no longer working.

¹This use is approved under FDA’s [Accelerated Approval Program](https://www.fda.gov/ForPatients/Approvals/Fast/ucm405447.htm). As a condition of approval, [confirmatory trial(s)](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=799146&version=Patient&language=English) must show that selpercatinib provides a clinical benefit in these patients.

Selpercatinib
is also being studied in the treatment of other types of
cancer.
