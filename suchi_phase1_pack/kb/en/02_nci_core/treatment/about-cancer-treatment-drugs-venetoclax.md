## Use in Cancer

Venetoclax
is approved to treat:

- **[Acute myeloid leukemia](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46347&version=Patient&language=English)** that is newly diagnosed. It is used in adults aged 75 years and older or adults who cannot be treated with [intensive](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=796903&version=Patient&language=English) [induction chemotherapy](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45736&version=Patient&language=English). It is given with either [azacitidine](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45177&version=Patient&language=English), [decitabine](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45208&version=Patient&language=English), or [low-dose](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=792090&version=Patient&language=English) [cytarabine](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45155&version=Patient&language=English).
- **[Chronic lymphocytic leukemia](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44846&version=Patient&language=English)** (CLL) or **[small lymphocytic lymphoma](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=407751&version=Patient&language=English)** (SLL) in adults. It may be given alone or with either [obinutuzumab](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=754339&version=Patient&language=English) or [rituximab](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45525&version=Patient&language=English).

Venetoclax
is also being studied in the treatment of other types of
cancer.
