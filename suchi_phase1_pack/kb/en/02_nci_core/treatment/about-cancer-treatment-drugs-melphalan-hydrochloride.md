## Use in Cancer

Melphalan hydrochloride
is approved to treat:

- **[Multiple myeloma](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45793&version=Patient&language=English).** This use is approved for the Evomela brand of melphalan hydrochloride.
- It is used as [palliative treatment](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45815&version=Patient&language=English) in patients who cannot take the tablet form of melphalan.
- It is used as a [conditioning treatment](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=549700&version=Patient&language=English) to prepare patients for a [stem cell transplant](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46695&version=Patient&language=English).
- **[Uveal melanoma](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=269467&version=Patient&language=English)** that has spread to the liver and cannot be removed by surgery. It is used in adults whose liver [metastases](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46710&version=Patient&language=English) affect less than half of the liver and either the cancer has not spread to other areas of the body or it has spread to the bone, [lymph nodes](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45762&version=Patient&language=English), [subcutaneous](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45914&version=Patient&language=English) tissue, or lungs and these metastases can be treated by surgery or radiation therapy. This use is approved for the Hepzato brand of melphalan hydrochloride.

Melphalan hydrochloride
is an injection form of melphalan. Melphalan also comes as a tablet that is taken by mouth. For more information about the tablet, see the Drug Information Summary for [Melphalan](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/melphalan).

Melphalan hydrochloride is also being studied in the treatment of other types of
cancer.
