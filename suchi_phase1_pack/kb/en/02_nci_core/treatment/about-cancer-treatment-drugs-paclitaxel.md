# Paclitaxel - NCI

# Paclitaxel

Placeholder slot

(PA-klih-TAK-sil)

Paclitaxel is a type of chemotherapy called a taxane. Taxanes interfere with microtubules (cellular structures that help move chromosomes during mitosis, or cell division). This stops mitosis and eventually causes cells to die.

## Use in Cancer

Paclitaxel is approved to be used alone or with other drugs to treat:

- **[AIDS-related](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=444964&version=Patient&language=English) [Kaposi sarcoma](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45134&version=Patient&language=English).** It is used as [second-line therapy](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=346513&version=Patient&language=English).
- **[Breast cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=444971&version=Patient&language=English).** It is used:
- as [adjuvant therapy](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45587&version=Patient&language=English) with [doxorubicin hydrochloride](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=560140&version=Patient&language=English)-containing [combination chemotherapy](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45650&version=Patient&language=English), in patients with [node-positive](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44937&version=Patient&language=English) cancer
- in patients with [metastatic](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44058&version=Patient&language=English) cancer that did not [respond](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44085&version=Patient&language=English) to combination chemotherapy
- **[Non-small cell lung cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45323&version=Patient&language=English).** It is used with [cisplatin](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45230&version=Patient&language=English) as [first-line therapy](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=346494&version=Patient&language=English) in patients whose cancer cannot be treated with surgery or [radiation therapy](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44971&version=Patient&language=English).
- **[Ovarian cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=445074&version=Patient&language=English)** that is [advanced](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=478743&version=Patient&language=English). It is used with cisplatin as first-line therapy or alone in patients who have already received other treatment.

Paclitaxel is also being studied in the treatment of other types of cancer.

Paclitaxel is also available in a different form called [paclitaxel albumin-stabilized nanoparticle formulation](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=507600&version=Patient&language=English). For more information, see the Drug Information Summary for [Paclitaxel Albumin-stabilized Nanoparticle Formulation](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/nanoparticlepaclitaxel).
