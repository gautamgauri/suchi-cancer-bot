# Mitoxantrone Hydrochloride - NCI

# Mitoxantrone Hydrochloride

Placeholder slot

(MY-toh-ZAN-trone HY-droh-KLOR-ide)

Mitoxantrone hydrochloride is a type of chemotherapy drug called an anthracycline. It blocks an enzyme called topoisomerase II, which cuts and repairs tangled strands of DNA. This stops or slows the growth of cancer cells and other rapidly dividing cells and causes them to die.

## Use in Cancer

Mitoxantrone hydrochloride
is approved
to be used with other drugs to treat:

- **[Acute non-lymphocytic leukemia](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46215&version=Patient&language=English)** in adults.
- **[Prostate cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=445079&version=Patient&language=English).** It is used as [palliative treatment](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45815&version=Patient&language=English) in patients with [advanced](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=478743&version=Patient&language=English) disease that is [hormone-refractory](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=686077&version=Patient&language=English) (does not [respond](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44085&version=Patient&language=English) to [hormone treatment](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44525&version=Patient&language=English)).

Mitoxantrone hydrochloride
is also being studied in the treatment of other types of
cancer.
