# VeIP - NCI

# VeIP

This chemotherapy combination contains the drugs vinblastine sulfate (Velban), ifosfamide, and cisplatin (Platinol). Combinations usually work better than single drugs because
different drugs kill
cancer
cells in different ways.

Drugs in the VeIP combination:

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Ve** | = [Vinblastine Sulfate](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/vinblastinesulfate) (Velban) |
| **I** | = [Ifosfamide](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/ifosfamide) |
| **P** | = [Cisplatin](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/cisplatin) (Platinol) |

Each of the drugs in this combination is approved by the
[Food and Drug
Administration (FDA)](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=454785&version=Patient&language=English)
to treat cancer or conditions related to cancer.

## Use in Cancer

VeIP
is used to treat [advanced](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=478743&version=Patient&language=English) disease in:

- **[Ovarian germ cell](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=446560&version=Patient&language=English)** cancer.
- **[Testicular cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=445090&version=Patient&language=English).**

This combination may also be used with other drugs or treatments or to treat other types of cancer.
