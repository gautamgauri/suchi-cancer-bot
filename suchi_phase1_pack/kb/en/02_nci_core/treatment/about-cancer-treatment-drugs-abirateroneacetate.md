## Use in Cancer

Abiraterone acetate is approved to be used with [prednisone](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45492&version=Patient&language=English) to treat:

- **[Prostate cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=445079&version=Patient&language=English)** that has [metastasized](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46283&version=Patient&language=English) (spread to other parts of the body). It is used:
- In patients whose [cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45333&version=Patient&language=English) is [castrate resistant](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=753525&version=Patient&language=English) (has not responded to treatments that lower [testosterone](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45581&version=Patient&language=English) levels). This use is approved for the Zytiga brand name of abiraterone acetate.
- With [methylprednisolone](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45370&version=Patient&language=English) in patients whose cancer is castrate resistant. This use is approved for the Yonsa brand name of abiraterone acetate.
- In patients whose cancer is [high-risk](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45079&version=Patient&language=English) and [castrate sensitive](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=804591&version=Patient&language=English) (has responded to treatments that lower testosterone levels). This use is approved for the Zytiga brand name of abiraterone acetate.

Abiraterone
is also being studied in the treatment of other types of
cancer.
