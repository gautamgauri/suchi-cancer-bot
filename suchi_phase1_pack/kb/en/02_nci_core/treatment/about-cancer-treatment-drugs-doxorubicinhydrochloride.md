# Doxorubicin Hydrochloride - NCI

# Doxorubicin Hydrochloride

Placeholder slot

(DOK-soh-ROO-bih-sin HY-droh-KLOR-ide)

Doxorubicin hydrochloride is a type of antibiotic called an anthracycline that is used as a chemotherapy drug. It blocks an enzyme called topoisomerase II. This stops or slows the growth of cancer cells and other rapidly growing cells by damaging their DNA.

FDA Approved

Yes

## Use in Cancer

Doxorubicin hydrochloride is approved to be used alone or with other drugs to treat:

- **[acute lymphoblastic leukemia](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45586&version=Patient&language=English)** (ALL)
- **[acute myeloid leukemia](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46347&version=Patient&language=English)** (AML)
- **[breast cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=444971&version=Patient&language=English).** It is used:
- with other drugs after surgery to remove the [primary tumor](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45847&version=Patient&language=English) in women whose cancer has spread to the [lymph nodes](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45762&version=Patient&language=English) under the arm
- in patients with [metastatic](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44058&version=Patient&language=English) breast cancer
- **[gastric (stomach) cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=454513&version=Patient&language=English)** that is metastatic
- **[Hodgkin lymphoma](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=270800&version=Patient&language=English)**
- **[neuroblastoma](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45418&version=Patient&language=English)** that is metastatic
- **[non-Hodgkin lymphoma](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45148&version=Patient&language=English)**
- **[non-small cell lung cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45327&version=Patient&language=English)** that is metastatic
- **[ovarian cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=445074&version=Patient&language=English)** that is metastatic
- **[small cell lung cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45327&version=Patient&language=English)** that is metastatic
- **[soft tissue](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46584&version=Patient&language=English) and bone [sarcomas](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45562&version=Patient&language=English)** that are metastatic
- **[thyroid cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=445091&version=Patient&language=English)** that is metastatic
- **[transitional cell](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46629&version=Patient&language=English) [bladder cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=444968&version=Patient&language=English)** that is metastatic
- **[Wilms tumor](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45946&version=Patient&language=English)** that is metastatic

Doxorubicin hydrochloride is also being studied in the treatment of other types of cancer.

Doxorubicin hydrochloride is also available in a different form called [doxorubicin hydrochloride liposome](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=560136&version=Patient&language=English). For more information, see the Drug Information Summary for [Doxorubicin Hydrochloride Liposome](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/doxorubicin-hydrochloride-liposome).
