# BEP - NCI

# BEP

This chemotherapy combination contains the drugs bleomycin, etoposide phosphate, and cisplatin (Platinol). Combinations usually work better than single drugs because
different drugs kill
cancer
cells in different ways.

Drugs in the BEP combination:

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **B** | = [Bleomycin](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/bleomycin) |
| **E** | = [Etoposide Phosphate](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/etoposidephosphate) |
| **P** | = [Cisplatin](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/cisplatin) (Platinol) |

Each of the
drugs
in this
combination
is approved by the
[Food and Drug
Administration (FDA)](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=454785&version=Patient&language=English)
to treat cancer or conditions related to
cancer.

## Use in Cancer

BEP is used to treat:

- **[Ovarian germ cell tumors](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=446560&version=Patient&language=English)** that are [malignant](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45772&version=Patient&language=English).
- **[Testicular germ cell tumors](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=695185&version=Patient&language=English)** that are malignant.

This combination may also be used with other drugs or treatments or to treat other types of cancer.
