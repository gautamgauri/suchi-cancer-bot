## Use in Cancer

Cytarabine is approved to be used with other [drugs](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=348921&version=Patient&language=English) to treat:

- **[Acute non-lymphocytic leukemia](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46215&version=Patient&language=English)** in adults and children.

Cytarabine is also approved to [prevent](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=439419&version=Patient&language=English) and treat:

- **[Meningeal leukemia](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=670768&version=Patient&language=English)** ([leukemia](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45343&version=Patient&language=English) that has spread to the [meninges](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46295&version=Patient&language=English)). It is given as [intrathecal therapy](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46681&version=Patient&language=English).

Cytarabine may also be used to treat:

- **[Acute lymphoblastic leukemia](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45586&version=Patient&language=English)** (ALL).
- **[Chronic myelogenous leukemia](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44901&version=Patient&language=English)** (CML) in [blastic phase](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46113&version=Patient&language=English).

Cytarabine is also being studied in the treatment of other types of [cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45333&version=Patient&language=English).

Cytarabine is also available in a different form, combined with Daunorubicin Hydrochloride. For more information see the Drug Information Summary for [Daunorubicin Hydrochloride and Cytarabine Liposome](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/daunorubicinhydrochlorideandcytarabineliposome).
