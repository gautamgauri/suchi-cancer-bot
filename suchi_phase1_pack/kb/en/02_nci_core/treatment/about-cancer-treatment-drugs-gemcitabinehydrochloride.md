# Gemcitabine Hydrochloride - NCI

# Gemcitabine Hydrochloride

Placeholder slot

(jem-SY-tuh-been HY-droh-KLOR-ide)

Gemcitabine hydrochloride is a type of chemotherapy drug called an antimetabolite, which is a drug that mimics a natural chemical and prevents its use in cells. It disrupts DNA formation by mimicking a DNA building block. This slows or stops the growth of cancer cells and other rapidly growing cells.

## Use in Cancer

Gemcitabine hydrochloride is approved to be used alone or with other drugs to treat:

- **[Breast cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=444971&version=Patient&language=English)** that cannot be treated with or that did not respond to chemotherapy that included an [anthracycline](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44916&version=Patient&language=English) drug. It is used with [paclitaxel](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45258&version=Patient&language=English) as the first therapy for cancer that has spread to other parts of the body.
- **[Non-small cell lung cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45323&version=Patient&language=English).** It is used with [cisplatin](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45230&version=Patient&language=English) in patients whose cancer is [stage IIIA](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45367&version=Patient&language=English), [stage IIIB](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=682421&version=Patient&language=English), or [stage IV](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45144&version=Patient&language=English).
- **[Ovarian cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=445074&version=Patient&language=English)** that is advanced and has relapsed at least 6 months after treatment with [platinum](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45841&version=Patient&language=English) chemotherapy. It is used with [carboplatin](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45182&version=Patient&language=English).
- **[Pancreatic cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44521&version=Patient&language=English).** It is used alone in patients whose cancer is [stage II](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45490&version=Patient&language=English) or [stage III](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45497&version=Patient&language=English) and cannot be removed by surgery or [stage IV](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45511&version=Patient&language=English). It is used in patients whose cancer has been treated with [fluorouracil](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46090&version=Patient&language=English).

Gemcitabine hydrochloride is also being studied in the treatment of other types of cancer.
