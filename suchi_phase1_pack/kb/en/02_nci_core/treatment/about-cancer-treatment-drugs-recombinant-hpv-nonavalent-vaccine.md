# Recombinant Human Papillomavirus (HPV) Nonavalent Vaccine - NCI

# Recombinant Human Papillomavirus (HPV) Nonavalent Vaccine

Placeholder slot

(ree-KOM-bih-nunt HYOO-mun PA-pih-LOH-muh-VY-rus NOH-nuh-VAY-lent vak-SEEN)

The HPV nonavalent vaccine protects against infection with low-risk HPV types 6 and 11, which cause most genital warts, and against high-risk HPV types 16, 18, 31, 33, 45, 52, and 58, which cause several types of precancers and cancer.

US Brand Name(s)

Gardasil 9

FDA Approved

Yes

[FDA label information for this drug is available at DailyMed.](https://dailymed.nlm.nih.gov/dailymed/drugInfo.cfm?setid=a21f4f4b-b891-4f25-b747-cb9ec7d865d6&audience=consumer)

## Use in Cancer

The HPV nonavalent vaccine is used in individuals aged 9 to 45 years to prevent:

- **[Anal cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=444966&version=Patient&language=English).**
- **[Cervical cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=444973&version=Patient&language=English).**
- **[Head and neck cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=257519&version=Patient&language=English)**, including [oropharyngeal cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=446523&version=Patient&language=English).¹
- **[Vaginal cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=523460&version=Patient&language=English).**
- **[Vulvar cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44776&version=Patient&language=English).**
- **[Lesions](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46324&version=Patient&language=English)** that sometimes lead to anal, cervical, vaginal, or vulvar cancer.
- **[Genital warts](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=409765&version=Patient&language=English).**

¹This use is approved under FDA’s [Accelerated Approval Program](https://www.fda.gov/ForPatients/Approvals/Fast/ucm405447.htm). As a condition of approval, [confirmatory trial(s)](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=799146&version=Patient&language=English) must show that recombinant human papillomavirus (HPV) nonavalent vaccine provides a clinical benefit in these patients.
