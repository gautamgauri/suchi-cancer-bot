# Recombinant Human Papillomavirus (HPV) Quadrivalent Vaccine

Placeholder slot

(ree-KOM-bih-nunt HYOO-mun PA-pih-LOH-muh-VY-rus KWAH-drih-VAY-lent vak-SEEN)

The HPV quadrivalent vaccine protects against infection with low-risk HPV types 6 and 11, which cause most genital warts, and against high-risk HPV types 16 and 18, which cause several types of precancers and cancer.

US Brand Name(s)

Gardasil

FDA Approved

No longer available in the U.S.

## Use in Cancer

The HPV quadrivalent vaccine is used in individuals aged 9 to 26 years to prevent:

- **[Anal cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=444966&version=Patient&language=English).**
- **[Cervical cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=444973&version=Patient&language=English).**
- **[Vaginal cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=523460&version=Patient&language=English).**
- **[Vulvar cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44776&version=Patient&language=English).**
- **[Lesions](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46324&version=Patient&language=English)** that sometimes lead to anal, cervical, vaginal, or vulvar cancer.
- **[Genital warts](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=409765&version=Patient&language=English).**

The recombinant HPV quadrivalent vaccine is no longer available in the United States. It is still used in other countries.
