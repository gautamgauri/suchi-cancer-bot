# Prednisone - NCI

# Prednisone

Placeholder slot

(PRED-nih-sone)

Prednisone is a synthetic corticosteroid (a type of steroid hormone). It reduces inflammation and lowers the body’s immune response, preventing the immune system from attacking the body’s own tissues.

FDA Approved

Yes

[FDA label information for this drug is available at DailyMed.](https://dailymed.nlm.nih.gov/dailymed/search.cfm?labeltype=all&query=PREDNISONE&pagesize=20&page=1&audience=consumer)

## Use in Cancer

Prednisone is approved to be used to reduce [inflammation](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44042&version=Patient&language=English) and suppress (lower) the body's [immune response](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45722&version=Patient&language=English).

It is used with other drugs to treat the following types of cancer:

- **[Acute lymphoblastic leukemia](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45586&version=Patient&language=English)** (ALL) and **[acute myeloid leukemia](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46347&version=Patient&language=English)** (AML). It is used as [palliative therapy](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45815&version=Patient&language=English) in adults and children.
- **[Chronic lymphocytic leukemia](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44846&version=Patient&language=English)** (CLL) and **[chronic myelogenous leukemia](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44901&version=Patient&language=English)** (CML). It is used as palliative therapy in adults.
- **[Hodgkin lymphoma](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=270800&version=Patient&language=English).** It is used as palliative therapy in adults.
- **[Mycosis fungoides](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45794&version=Patient&language=English)** (a type of [cutaneous T-cell lymphoma](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46771&version=Patient&language=English)).
- **[Non-Hodgkin lymphoma](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45148&version=Patient&language=English).** It is used as palliative therapy in adults.

Prednisone is also used alone or with other drugs to [prevent](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=439419&version=Patient&language=English) or treat the following conditions related to cancer:

- **[Anemia](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45360&version=Patient&language=English).**
- **[Drug hypersensitivity](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45715&version=Patient&language=English)** ([allergic reactions](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=443292&version=Patient&language=English)).
- **[Hypercalcemia](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45363&version=Patient&language=English)** (high blood levels of [calcium](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45632&version=Patient&language=English)).
- **[Thrombocytopenia](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45365&version=Patient&language=English)** (low [platelet](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45840&version=Patient&language=English) levels) in adults.

Prednisone is also used alone or with other drugs to treat many other diseases and conditions. The drug continues to be studied in the treatment of many types of cancer and other conditions.
