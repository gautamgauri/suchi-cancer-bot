# COPP

This combination contains the chemotherapy drugs cyclophosphamide, vincristine sulfate (Oncovin), and procarbazine hydrochloride and the steroid hormone prednisone. Combinations usually work better than single drugs because
different drugs kill
cancer
cells in different ways.

Drugs in the COPP combination:

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **C** | = [Cyclophosphamide](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/cyclophosphamide) |
| **O** | = [Vincristine Sulfate](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/vincristinesulfate) (Oncovin) |
| **P** | = [Procarbazine Hydrochloride](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/procarbazinehydrochloride) |
| **P** | = [Prednisone](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/prednisone) |

Each of the
drugs
in this
combination
is approved by the
[Food and Drug
Administration (FDA)](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=454785&version=Patient&language=English)
to treat
cancer
or conditions related to cancer.

## Use in Cancer

COPP is used to treat:

- **[Hodgkin lymphoma](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=270800&version=Patient&language=English).**
- **[Non-Hodgkin lymphoma](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45148&version=Patient&language=English)** (NHL).

This combination may also be used with other drugs or treatments or to treat other types of cancer.
