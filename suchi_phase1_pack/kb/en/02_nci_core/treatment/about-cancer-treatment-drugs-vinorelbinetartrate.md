(vih-NOR-el-been TAR-trayt)

Vincristine is a type of chemotherapy called a vinca alkaloid. Vinca alkaloids interfere with microtubules (cellular structures that help move chromosomes during mitosis). They block cell growth by stopping mitosis (cell division).

## Use in Cancer

Vinorelbine
tartrate is approved to treat:

- **[Non-small cell lung cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45323&version=Patient&language=English).** It is used as [first-line therapy](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=346494&version=Patient&language=English):
- With [cisplatin](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45230&version=Patient&language=English) in patients with [locally advanced](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45955&version=Patient&language=English) disease.
- Alone or with cisplatin in patients whose disease has spread to other parts of the body.

Vinorelbine tartrate is also being studied in the treatment of other types of cancer.
