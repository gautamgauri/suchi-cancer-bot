## Use in Cancer

Azacitidine is approved to treat:

- **[Acute myeloid leukemia](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46347&version=Patient&language=English)** in adults who had a first [complete remission](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45651&version=Patient&language=English) after [intensive](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=796903&version=Patient&language=English) [induction therapy](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45736&version=Patient&language=English) and who are not able to finish intensive curative therapy. This use is approved for the Onureg brand of azacitidine.
- **[Myelodysplastic syndromes](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45266&version=Patient&language=English)** (MDS).
- It is used in adults with certain types of myelodysplastic syndromes, including [chronic myelomonocytic leukemia](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=367436&version=Patient&language=English).
- It is used in children aged 1 month and older with newly diagnosed [juvenile myelomonocytic leukemia](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45048&version=Patient&language=English).This use is approved for the Vidaza brand of azacitidine.

Azacitidine is also being studied in the treatment of other conditions and types of [cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45333&version=Patient&language=English).
