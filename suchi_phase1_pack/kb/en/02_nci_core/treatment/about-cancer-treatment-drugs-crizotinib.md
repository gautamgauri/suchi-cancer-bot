## Use in Cancer

Crizotinib
is approved
to treat:

- **[Anaplastic large cell lymphoma](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45552&version=Patient&language=English)** that is [ALK positive](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=796880&version=Patient&language=English) and [systemic](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44177&version=Patient&language=English). It is used in children aged 1 year and older and young adults whose cancer has [relapsed](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45866&version=Patient&language=English) (come back) or is [refractory](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45863&version=Patient&language=English) (does not respond to treatment).
- **[Inflammatory myofibroblastic tumor](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=782642&version=Patient&language=English)** that is ALK positive. It is used in adults and children aged 1 year and older whose cancer is relapsed, refractory, or cannot be removed by surgery.
- **[Non-small cell lung cancer](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45323&version=Patient&language=English)** that is ALK positive or [ROS1 positive](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=796946&version=Patient&language=English) and has spread to other parts of the body.

Crizotinib
is also being studied in the treatment of other types of
cancer.
