## Use in Cancer

Arsenic trioxide is approved to be used with [tretinoin](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45351&version=Patient&language=English) to treat:

- **[Acute promyelocytic leukemia](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=444957&version=Patient&language=English)** (APL) in patients whose cancer has a certain type of [chromosome](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46470&version=Patient&language=English) [mutation](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46063&version=Patient&language=English) that affects the *[PML](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=794088&version=Patient&language=English)* gene and *[RARA](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=794091&version=Patient&language=English)* gene. It is used:
- In adults with newly diagnosed APL that is [low risk](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=794093&version=Patient&language=English).
- In patients whose cancer has not gotten better or has [relapsed](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45866&version=Patient&language=English) after [retinoid](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45870&version=Patient&language=English) and [anthracycline](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44916&version=Patient&language=English) chemotherapy.

Arsenic trioxide is also being studied in the treatment of other types of cancer.
