# VAC

This chemotherapy combination contains the drugs vincristine sulfate, dactinomycin (Actinomycin-D), and cyclophosphamide. Combinations usually work better than single drugs because
different drugs kill
cancer
cells in different ways.

Drugs in the VAC combination:

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **V** | = [Vincristine Sulfate](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/vincristinesulfate) |
| **A** | = [Dactinomycin](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/dactinomycin) (Actinomycin-D) |
| **C** | = [Cyclophosphamide](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/cyclophosphamide) |

Each of the drugs in this combination is approved by the
[Food and Drug
Administration (FDA)](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=454785&version=Patient&language=English)
to treat cancer or conditions related to cancer.

## Use in Cancer

VAC
is used to treat:

- **[Ovarian germ cell tumors](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=446560&version=Patient&language=English)** (certain types).
- **[Rhabdomyosarcoma](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45872&version=Patient&language=English)** in children.

This combination may also be used with other drugs or
treatments or to treat other types of cancer.
