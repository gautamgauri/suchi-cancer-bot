# JEB - NCI

# JEB

This chemotherapy combination contains the drugs carboplatin (JM8), etoposide phosphate, and bleomycin. Combinations usually work better than single drugs because
different drugs kill
cancer
cells in different ways.

Drugs in the JEB combination:

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **J** | = [Carboplatin](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/carboplatin) (JM8) |
| **E** | = [Etoposide Phosphate](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/etoposidephosphate) |
| **B** | = [Bleomycin](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/bleomycin) |

Each of the drugs in this combination is approved by the
[Food and Drug
Administration (FDA)](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=454785&version=Patient&language=English)
to treat cancer or conditions related to cancer.

## Use in Cancer

JEB
is used in children to treat the following [malignant](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45772&version=Patient&language=English) [extracranial germ cell tumors](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=444992&version=Patient&language=English):

- **[Ovarian germ cell tumors](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=446560&version=Patient&language=English).**
- **[Testicular germ cell tumors](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=695185&version=Patient&language=English).**

This combination may also be used with other drugs or
treatments or to treat other types of cancer.
