# COPP-ABV

This combination contains the chemotherapy drugs cyclophosphamide, vincristine sulfate (Oncovin), procarbazine hydrochloride, doxorubicin hydrochloride (Adriamycin), bleomycin, and vinblastine sulfate and the steroid hormone prednisone. Combinations usually work better than single drugs because
different drugs kill
cancer
cells in different ways.

Drugs in the COPP-ABV combination:

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **C** | = [Cyclophosphamide](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/cyclophosphamide) |
| **O** | = [Vincristine Sulfate](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/vincristinesulfate) (Oncovin) |
| **P** | = [Prednisone](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/prednisone) |
| **P** | = [Procarbazine Hydrochloride](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/procarbazinehydrochloride) |
| **A** | = [Doxorubicin Hydrochloride](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/doxorubicinhydrochloride) (Adriamycin) |
| **B** | = [Bleomycin](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/bleomycin) |
| **V** | = [Vinblastine Sulfate](/about-cancer/treatment/drugs/vinblastinesulfate) |

Each of the drugs in this combination is approved by the
[Food and Drug
Administration (FDA)](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=454785&version=Patient&language=English)
to treat cancer or conditions related to cancer.

## Use in Cancer

COPP-ABV
is used to treat:

- **[Hodgkin lymphoma](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=270800&version=Patient&language=English)** in children. It may be used with [radiation therapy](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=44971&version=Patient&language=English).

This combination may also be used with other drugs or
treatments or to treat other types of cancer.
