# DDX41-Associated Myeloid Malignancies (PDQ®) - NCI

# DDX41-Associated Myeloid Malignancies (PDQ®)–Health Professional Version

## Introduction and Clinical Manifestations of DDX41-Associated Myeloid Malignancies

Germline [pathogenic variants](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=783960&version=healthprofessional&language=English&dictionary=genetic) in the RNA helicase, *DDX41*, are associated with an [autosomal dominant](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=793860&version=healthprofessional&language=English&dictionary=genetic) predisposition to myeloid malignancies and other cancers. The prevalence of *DDX41* germline pathogenic variants is not clearly defined. However, these variants are thought to underlie 1% to 1.5% of all acute myeloid leukemia (AML) diagnoses,[[1](#cit/section_1.1)] making it one of the most commonly [inherited predispositions](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=809371&version=healthprofessional&language=English&dictionary=genetic) to adult-onset myeloid malignancies.[[2](#cit/section_1.2)] Individuals who inherit *DDX41* pathogenic variants have an increased lifetime risk to develop myelodysplastic syndrome (MDS) and AML. However, lymphoid malignancies such as non-Hodgkin lymphoma and myeloproliferative neoplasm (MPN) have also been reported in individuals with *DDX41* pathogenic variants.[[3](#cit/section_1.3)-[5](#cit/section_1.5)]

*DDX41* germline pathogenic variants are associated with a later median age of MDS/AML diagnosis than other inherited hematologic [predisposition syndromes](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=754220&version=healthprofessional&language=English&dictionary=genetic).[[6](#cit/section_1.6)] For *DDX41* [carriers](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=460132&version=healthprofessional&language=English&dictionary=genetic), the age of MDS/AML diagnosis can range from 61 to 69 years. This age range is similar to the typical age of diagnosis in patients with [sporadic](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=339347&version=healthprofessional&language=English&dictionary=genetic) MDS/AML in the general population. The myeloid malignancies that occur in individuals with *DDX41* germline pathogenic variants are almost always diploid and harbor lower somatic mutational burdens than those seen in individuals without *DDX41* germline pathogenic variants.[[4](#cit/section_1.4),[6](#cit/section_1.6)] The [penetrance](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=339344&version=healthprofessional&language=English&dictionary=genetic) of *DDX41* pathogenic variants is incomplete and variable. *DDX41* pathogenic variants are associated with a mild to moderate predisposition to hematologic malignancies (lifetime risk of hematologic malignancies ranges from 20% to 30% in *DDX41* carriers). Nearly half of reported *DDX41* carriers develop long-standing asymptomatic cytopenias (most commonly leukopenia) prior to malignancy development. However, some *DDX41* carriers will only ever develop stable cytopenias that never progress to become hematologic malignancies. In these cases, individuals are typically transfusion-independent.[[2](#cit/section_1.2)]

Since *DDX41* pathogenic variants have low penetrance and *DDX41*-related hematologic malignancies are often diagnosed at later ages, *DDX41* carriers can be difficult to identify using the typical age– or family history–related [screening](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46171&version=healthprofessional&language=English&dictionary=genetic) criteria.

## Genetics and Molecular Biology of DDX41-Associated Myeloid Malignancies

*DDX41* [somatic variants](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=781854&version=healthprofessional&language=English&dictionary=genetic) occur in myeloid and lymphoid malignancies and are increasingly included as part of somatic [next-generation sequencing](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=763024&version=healthprofessional&language=English&dictionary=genetic) (NGS) panels. Studies have shown that molecular profiling is a useful and feasible tool to [screen](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46171&version=healthprofessional&language=English&dictionary=genetic) patients with hematologic malignancies for [germline](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=460154&version=healthprofessional&language=English&dictionary=genetic) *DDX41* [pathogenic variants](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=783960&version=healthprofessional&language=English&dictionary=genetic).[[1](#cit/section_2.1),[2](#cit/section_2.2)] Two common Northern European [founder pathogenic variants](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=783962&version=healthprofessional&language=English&dictionary=genetic), p.Asp140Glyfs\*2 and p.Met1Ile, account for most *DDX41* germline pathogenic [variants](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=776887&version=healthprofessional&language=English&dictionary=genetic) identified in individuals with European ancestry. As genetic testing becomes more routine, testing in additional populations indicates that [missense variants](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=783964&version=healthprofessional&language=English&dictionary=genetic), like *DDX41* p.Val152Gly (p.V152G) and *DDX41* p.Tyr259Cys (p.Y259C), may be more common in non-European populations.[[3](#cit/section_2.3)] When these missense and founder variants are found on somatic testing, they are present in the germline nearly 100% of the time. However, multiple [nonsense](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=783965&version=healthprofessional&language=English&dictionary=genetic) and [frameshift](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=783963&version=healthprofessional&language=English&dictionary=genetic) germline variants have been reported throughout the *DDX41* [gene](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=45693&version=healthprofessional&language=English&dictionary=genetic). Therefore, full *DDX41* gene sequencing is indicated when evaluating for a germline pathogenic variant.

In nearly 80% of cases, a second, somatically-acquired variant (often the *DDX41* p.Arg525His [p.R525H] variant), is acquired on the other *DDX41* [allele](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=339337&version=healthprofessional&language=English&dictionary=genetic). This second hit is associated with progression to hematologic malignancy.[[4](#cit/section_2.4),[5](#cit/section_2.5)] Likewise, if the *DDX41* p.Arg525His variant is detected on somatic testing, even in absence of an additional *DDX41* variant (presumably on the other allele), germline testing is warranted. Germline pathogenic variants on the other *DDX41* allele could be present, especially when full somatic sequencing of the gene is not performed.

## Management and Prognosis for DDX41-Associated Myeloid Malignancies

Emerging data suggest that *DDX41* [carriers](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=460132&version=healthprofessional&language=English&dictionary=genetic) who develop acute myeloid leukemia (AML) have higher complete remission rates and longer mean overall survival rates than individuals who do not carry a *DDX41* [pathogenic variant](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=783960&version=healthprofessional&language=English&dictionary=genetic).[[1](#cit/section_3.1)-[3](#cit/section_3.3)] In addition, individuals with *DDX41* [germline](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=460154&version=healthprofessional&language=English&dictionary=genetic) pathogenic variants who develop myelodysplastic syndrome (MDS)/AML may show responses to treatment with lenalidomide.[[3](#cit/section_3.3)-[6](#cit/section_3.6)]

At least two cases of donor-derived leukemias have occurred in *DDX41* carriers post–hematopoietic stem cell transplant (HSCT) from a matched-related donor carrying the same germline *DDX41* pathogenic [variant](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=776887&version=healthprofessional&language=English&dictionary=genetic).[[7](#cit/section_3.7),[8](#cit/section_3.8)] This highlights the need for systematic [screening](/Common/PopUps/popDefinition.aspx?id=46171&version=healthprofessional&language=English&dictionary=genetic) of germline *DDX41* pathogenic variants in MDS/AML patients prior to HSCT. This will allow for appropriate donor selection and donor screening.
