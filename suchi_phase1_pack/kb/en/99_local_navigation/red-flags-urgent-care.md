## Red flags (seek urgent care now)

If you experience any of these symptoms, seek immediate medical attention:

- **Severe shortness of breath or chest pain** - Can indicate serious complications
- **Fainting or confusion** - May signal neurological issues or severe infection
- **Uncontrolled bleeding** - Any bleeding that doesn't stop with pressure
- **Vomiting blood** - Requires immediate emergency care
- **High fever with severe weakness** - Especially dangerous during chemotherapy when immunity is low
- **Severe abdominal pain** - Can indicate various serious conditions
- **Signs of infection** - High fever, chills, rapid heartbeat (especially during treatment)

## What to do now

1. **Go to the nearest emergency department** - Don't wait if symptoms are severe
2. **Call local emergency services** - Use 108 (India) or your local emergency number
3. **Contact your oncology team** - If you're in treatment, notify them immediately
4. **Bring someone with you** - Have a family member or friend accompany you if possible

## Important notes

- Trust your instincts - if something feels wrong, seek help
- It's better to be cautious than to wait
- Keep emergency contact numbers easily accessible
- Have your medical history and current medications list ready
